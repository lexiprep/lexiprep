# CEFR wordlist attribution

Two bundled files map English lemmas to CEFR levels:

- **`en.csv`** — the **vetted** tier (A1–C2), from CEFR-J + Octanove (below).
- **`en-freq.csv`** — the **estimated** fallback tier (A1–C1), from frequency-derived
  levels (see "Frequency fallback" at the end). Used only for words the vetted tier
  misses; the seed loads the vetted tier first, so it always wins.

## `en.csv` — vetted tier

`en.csv` is a derived, combined wordlist mapping English lemmas to CEFR levels (A1–C2).
It is generated by `apps/server/scripts/build-cefr-csv.ts` from two openly-licensed
sources, both distributed via [openlanguageprofiles/olp-en-cefrj](https://github.com/openlanguageprofiles/olp-en-cefrj):

## A1–B2 — CEFR-J Vocabulary Profile (version 1.5)

- Compiled by **Yukio Tono**, Tono Laboratory, Tokyo University of Foreign Studies (TUFS).
- The CEFR-J Wordlist is free to use for research and commercial purposes at no charge,
  **provided the dataset is cited**.
- Cite: *CEFR-J Wordlist Version 1.5. Compiled by Yukio Tono, Tokyo University of Foreign
  Studies. https://cefr-j.org/*

## C1–C2 — Octanove Vocabulary Profile (C1/C2, version 1.0)

## `en-freq.csv` — frequency fallback tier

`en-freq.csv` maps English surface words to an **estimated** CEFR level (A1–C1 only) and
is generated by `apps/server/scripts/build-cefr-freq-csv.ts`. It is the second, lower-
priority tier: levels are estimated from overall English frequency, used only to fill
words absent from the vetted `en.csv`.

- Derived from **[Maximax67/Words-CEFR-Dataset](https://github.com/Maximax67/Words-CEFR-Dataset)**
  (**MIT License**), which computes per-word CEFR levels from the **CEFR-J** dataset
  (cited above) combined with **Google Books N-gram** 1-gram frequency.
- We keep only **A1–C1** and drop the source's C2 band — in that dataset C2 is a catch-all
  for rare/unknown words (~74% of entries), not a vetted advanced grade.
- This file contains **no** CC BY-SA (Octanove) material, so the share-alike note above
  does not apply to it; it carries the MIT terms of its source plus the CEFR-J citation.
